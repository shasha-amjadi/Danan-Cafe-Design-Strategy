# Danan — Color Rules

## The Palette

| Name | Role |
|---|---|
| Terracotta | Primary identity color |
| Mocha | Deep, mature tone |
| Beige / Oatmeal | Base and breathing space |
| Sage Green | Seasonal and nature accent |
| Amber / Ochre | Structural and sectioning color |

---

## Core Rules

### 1. Two Active Colors Per Frame
Never use more than two palette colors as active elements in a single frame.
Textures (craft paper, terracotta wall) are **not counted** as active colors — they are backgrounds.
This means two active colors can appear on top of a texture without breaking this rule.

### 2. Color Dominance — 60 / 40
In every frame, one color must be dominant and the other supporting.
A 50/50 split flattens hierarchy and weakens visual focus.

### 3. Approved Pairings

| Pairing | Mood | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Terracotta + Beige | Warm, authoritative, primary | Festive posts, highlight covers, coasters |
| Mocha + Beige | Deep, calm, classic | Menu body, text posts, packaging paper |
| Sage Green + Beige | Natural, fresh, soothing | Seasonal content, nature-adjacent posts |
| Mocha + Terracotta | Rich, mature, formal | Heavy typography, formal announcements |

### 4. Forbidden Pairing
**Sage Green + Terracotta** — excessive contrast, breaks brand calm.

---

## Amber / Ochre — Structural Color

Amber is a **structural color**, not a decorative one.

Use it where you need to separate or categorize — menu tabs, section dividers, packaging categories.

**Amber is appropriate in:**
- Menu section tabs (as seen in the arch-shaped menu design)
- Packaging category markers
- Pattern work alongside terracotta or mocha

**Amber must not appear in:**
- Body text or running typography
- Full-bleed Instagram backgrounds
- Any composition alongside Sage Green

---

## Typography Color Rule

Body text must always be **Beige or Mocha** only.
Never use Sage Green, Amber, or Terracotta for running text or small-scale copy.
Headlines and display text may use Terracotta or Mocha depending on background.

---

## Sage Green — Use With Restraint

Sage Green is a reserved color.
Its power comes from scarcity — the less it appears, the more it lands.
Limit its use to seasonal moments, nature-related content, or as a deliberate contrast pause in the visual feed.

---

## Texture Is Not a Color

Craft paper texture and terracotta wall texture are environmental backgrounds.
They set the stage — they do not count toward the two-color limit.
Always treat texture as the ground, never as a competing active element.
